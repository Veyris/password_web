The Summary ("the fine print")

Scientists found out, that more complex passwords (digits, small letters, capital letters, symbols) are often easier to guess and harder to memorize - mostly because of human behaviour (lazyness - or "the easy way"). As an example say "someone"'s favorite password is "password". To fullfill requirements of having at least one digit, one small letters, one capital letter and one symbol, "someone"'s password changed to "Pa$$word1" because - you know - it is the easy way. And this ... is bad. Hackers have so called "Wordlists" which contain Millions and more of passwords. With that, they are trying to "guess" Passwords of Accounts. Different Attack-Patterns make it possible to automatically change specific characters with others (like "s" to "$"). Make an experiment. Use the Password-Strength-Check on this website first by entering the password "password" and after that by entering the password "Pa$$word1". Compare both. What do you think? Do you think "Pa§§word1" is safer then 4 random words which are concenated, like "fishspaghettiteacherapple". Try all of them and see the results (especially times to Guess!)

Maybe you realized, that the second password was safer (more scored) then the first one. But did you see the time it needs to crack the password? Think about 18 Minutes to guess a password (Online (none-throttled)). Thats no time! Did you checked the Guessing Time of the Password with four concenated words? Awesome right?
